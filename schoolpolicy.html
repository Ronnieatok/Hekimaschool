School Policies

Guidelines for a safe and productive learning environment

Academic Policies

Grading System

  • A (90-100%) - Outstanding achievement
  • B (80-89%) - Above average achievement
  • C (70-79%) - Average achievement
  • D (60-69%) - Below average achievement
  • F (Below 60%) - Failing

Homework Policy

Students are expected to complete assignments on time. Late work will be accepted with a 10% reduction per day up to 3 days late.

Academic Integrity Policy +

Bright Future Academy maintains high standards of academic honesty. Violations include:

  • Plagiarism in any form
  • Unauthorized collaboration
  • Cheating on examinations
  • Falsification of data

Consequences: First offense results in a zero on the assignment and parental notification. Repeated offenses may lead to suspension.

Behavioral Policies

Code of Conduct

All students are expected to demonstrate respect, responsibility, and integrity at all times.

Disciplinary Procedures +
  1. Verbal Warning - For minor infractions
  2. Written Warning - Documented notice to parents
  3. Detention - After-school disciplinary session
  4. Suspension - Temporary removal from school
  5. Expulsion - Permanent removal for severe violations
Anti-Bullying Policy +

Bright Future Academy has zero tolerance for bullying in any form:

  • Physical aggression or threats
  • Verbal harassment
  • Cyberbullying
  • Social exclusion

All incidents will be investigated promptly and appropriate disciplinary action taken.

Attendance Policies

Daily Attendance

  • Students must arrive by 8:15 AM
  • More than 3 unexcused absences per term may result in retention
  • Parents must notify the school before 8:30 AM for absences

Tardiness

Students arriving after 8:15 AM must check in at the office. Three tardies equal one absence.

Health & Safety Policies

Illness Policy +

  • Contagious rash

Medication Policy +
  • All medications must be stored in the nurse's office
  • Prescription medications require doctor's authorization
  • Students may not carry medication without approval
  • Emergency medications (e.g., epipens) may be carried with proper documentation

Technology Policies

Acceptable Use

  • Devices may only be used for educational purposes
  • Students must follow teacher instructions regarding technology use
  • School network use is monitored and filtered

Prohibited Activities

  • Accessing inappropriate content
  • Using another student's account
  • Downloading unauthorized software
  • Cyberbullying or harassment
